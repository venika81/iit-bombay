Melghat Tiger Reserve
Melghat Tiger Reserve
The Melghat Tiger Reserve is a 2,768.52 square kilometer tropical dry deciduous forest in the northern part of Maharashtra's Amravati District. The reserve is home to 80 tigers, but sightings are rare. The reserve was established as a wildlife sanctuary in 1967 and became a tiger reserve in 1974. It was one of the first nine tiger reserves in India to be notified under Project Tiger, a wildlife conservation project that began in 1972.The reserve is dominated by teak, but also includes other species like tiwas, bija, haldu, saja, dhawda, ain, moha, tendu, achar, amla, behada, bhilwa, bor, mango, khair, jamun, apta, bel, and kulu. In total, the reserve has 769 recorded plant species. Some say the reserve is a beautiful, peaceful place. Others say it's a good place for kids' birthdays and kid-friendly hikes.

Amravati Fort
Amravati, a city in the state of Maharashtra, India, has a rich and vibrant history that dates back to the 11th century. The city was originally part of the Deogiri Kingdom in the 13th century. However, due to the dense forests, mountain ranges, and rugged terrains of the region, Amravati was sparsely populated. Despite this, it was a famous pilgrimage center, and people from near and far came to pay homage to Goddess Ambadevi.In 1722, Amravati, then known as Bhosle ki Amravati, was given to Shri Ranoji Bhosle by Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj. Gadgadeshwar Mandir
Gadgadeshwar Mandir
Gavi Gangadhareshwara Temple, also known as the Gavipura Cave Temple, located in Bengaluru, Karnataka, India. This temple is an example of Indian rock-cut architecture and is famous for its mysterious stone discs in the forecourt and the exact planning allowing the sun to shine on the shrine during certain times of the year.The temple is believed to have been built by Gautama Maharshi and Bharadwaja Muni in the Vedic period. It was later renovated in the 16th century AD by Kempe Gowda, the founder of Bengaluru1. One of the oldest temples in Bengaluru, the Gavi Gangadhareshwara temple was built by Kempe Gowda in recognition after being released from a prison term of five years by Rama Raya.The temple is built in a natural cave in Gavipuram and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. The courtyard of the temple contains several monolithic sculptures. The main attractions of the Gavi Gangadhareshwara temple are two granite pillars that support the giant disk of the sun and moon, and two pillars having several carvings of Nandi in a sitting posture at the top. The temple complex has numerous shrines for various deities in addition to the main deity Gavi Gangadhareshwara1. On the occasion of Makar Sankranti, the temple witnesses a unique phenomenon in the evening where sunlight passes through an arc between the horns of Nandi and falls directly on the linga inside the cave, illuminating the interior idol for an hour.

Jaistambh Square
Jaistambh Square, also known as Victory Tower, is a historic site in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India. The square is surrounded by important monuments, including the tombs of Aurangzeb‚Äôs sons, the Jama Masjid, and the Chini Mahal Palace. The Jaistambh Square is a large open space with a large stone column. The stone column is decorated with ornate carvings, and the names of those who fell in the Battle of Salher are inscribed1.
