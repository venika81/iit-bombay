About Nashik

Nashik is an ancient holy city in Maharashtra, a state in western India. It’s known for its links to the “Ramayana” epic poem. On the Godavari River is Panchavati, a temple complex. Nearby, Lord Rama was thought to have bathed at Ram Kund water tank, today attended by Hindu devotees. Shri Kalaram Sansthan Mandir is an ancient shrine to Rama, while Rama and Sita are said to have worshipped at Sita Gufaa caves.

Shree Kalaram Temple
Shree Kalaram Temple
The Shree Kalaram Temple is a significant Hindu temple located in the Panchavati area of Nashik, Maharashtra, India. The temple is dedicated to the deity Rama, and its name derives from the black statue of Rama housed within it. The literal translation of “Kalaram” is "black Rama". The temple also houses the statues of the goddess Sita and the god Lakshmana1. Thousands of devotees visit the temple every day. The original temple, dedicated to an unknown deity, was very old, estimated to be from the Rashtrakuta Period (7th to 11th centuries). The current temple was funded by Sardar Rangarao Odhekar and was built around 178812. According to a story, Odhekar had a dream that the statue of Rama in black color was in the Godavari River. He retrieved the statue from the river and built the temple. The temple played a pivotal role in the Dalit movement in India. B. R. Ambedkar led a protest outside the temple on 2 March 1930, to allow Dalits into the temple. The temple is constructed completely out of black stones and has four entrance doors, one each facing East, West, South, and North. The summit of the Kalaram Mandir is made up of 32 tons of gold.
Shri Muktidham Mandir
Shri Muktidham Mandir
Shri Muktidham Mandir is a well-known temple located in Nashik, Maharashtra, India. It’s made from marble that was sourced from Rajasthan. The temple was established by a local industrialist named Mr. J.D. Chauhan-Bytco. The temple complex is unique because it houses replicas of all the Jyotirlingas, which have been built as per the dimensions of the original deities and have been sanctified by sending them to their respective pilgrimage centers. There is also a temple dedicated to Lord Krishna within the complex. The walls of the Krishna temple are beautifully decorated with paintings depicting scenes from the life of Krishna and the Mahabharata. These paintings were created by the noted painter Raghubir Mulgaonkar. The walls of the temple also have the entire chapters of the Bhagavad Gita inscribed on them. In addition to the replicas of all the Jyotirlingas and the temple of Sri Krishna, there are also idols of all major Hindu Gods and Goddesses like Vishnu, Laxmi, Rama, Laxman, Sita, Hanuman, Durga, Ganesha. The temple complex also houses a dharamshala, which can accommodate a large number of pilgrims. The Muktidham Temple is visited by a large number of Hindu devotees, especially during the Kumbh Mela.

Pandav Leni Caves
Pandav Leni Caves
Pandav Leni Caves, also known as Pandavleni Caves or Trirashmi Caves, are a group of ancient rock-cut caves located near Nashik in Maharashtra, India. These caves hold great historical and architectural significance. The Pandav Leni Caves date back to the 3rd century BCE and were carved out of a single basalt rock hill. They are believed to have been used as Buddhist monastic complexes and served as a place of meditation and worship for Buddhist monks. The caves are named after the Pandavas, the legendary figures from the Hindu epic Mahabharata, as there is a popular belief that the Pandavas stayed in these caves during their exile. However, historical evidence suggests that the caves were actually created much earlier, during the reign of the Satavahana dynasty. There are a total of 24 caves in the complex, each showcasing intricate carvings, sculptures, and inscriptions. The caves vary in size and design, with some featuring prayer halls, chaityas (prayer halls with a stupa), and viharas (monastic cells). The sculptures depict various Buddhist deities, scenes from the life of Buddha, and intricate architectural motifs. Visiting the Pandav Leni Caves offers a glimpse into the rich Buddhist heritage of the region. The serene surroundings and the panoramic view from the hill make it a popular destination for history enthusiasts, archaeology lovers, and spiritual seekers. It is advisable to wear comfortable footwear as there are steps leading up to the caves. Additionally, visitors are encouraged to maintain the sanctity of the site and follow any guidelines provided by the authorities. In summary, Pandav Leni Caves are an ancient rock-cut cave complex near Nashik, Maharashtra, known for their historical significance, architectural beauty, and association with Buddhism.

Ramkund

During festivals and auspicious occasions, Ramkund comes alive with vibrant celebrations and rituals. Harihar Fort
Harihar Fort
Harihar Fort, also known as Harshagad, is a magnificent historical fort located in the Nashik district of Maharashtra, India. Situated at an elevation of approximately 3,676 feet (1,120 meters) above sea level, this fort stands tall amidst the picturesque Sahyadri mountain range. The fort's architecture is truly remarkable, with its distinctive triangular shape and rock-cut steps that lead to the summit. The ascent to the top of Harihar Fort is an adventure in itself, as trekkers navigate through steep and rugged terrain, making it a popular destination for thrill-seekers and nature enthusiasts. As you make your way up, you'll encounter a series of carved niches along the rock-cut staircase, adding an artistic touch to the fort's structure. These niches were likely used for resting or storing essential items during the fort's heyday. At the pinnacle of Harihar Fort, you'll be rewarded with breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. On clear days, you can witness the beauty of the Sahyadri mountain range stretching as far as the eye can see. The fort also offers a mesmerizing view of the nearby Trimbakeshwar Temple, a revered Hindu pilgrimage site. Exploring the fort further, you'll come across a small temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, adding a spiritual element to the historical significance of Harihar Fort. Additionally, there is a water cistern that served as a vital source of water for the fort's inhabitants. Harihar Fort holds a significant place in history, believed to have been constructed during the Yadava dynasty. Its strategic location and architectural brilliance make it a testament to the engineering prowess of the bygone era. Visiting Harihar Fort is not only an opportunity to delve into the rich history of the region but also a chance to immerse yourself in the natural beauty of the Sahyadri mountains. However, it is advisable to undertake the trek with caution and preferably in the company of experienced trekkers or local guides, as the terrain can be challenging.
Sundar Narayan Mandir
Sundar Narayan Mandir
At a distance of 2 km from Nashik Central Bus Station, Sundarnarayan temple is situated at the corner of Ahillyabai Holkar Bridge near Ram Kund in Panchavati area of Nashik. The Sundarnarayan Temple is built by Gangadhar Yashwant Chandrachud in 1756. The presiding deity of the temple is Lord Vishnu as Sundarnarayan. According to legend, once the area was a place haunted by an evil demon named Jalandar who was an ardent devotee of Lord Shiva. Even though the demon was wild and practiced evil deeds, he had a pious and virtuous wife Vrinda Devi. Lord Shiva was highly impressed by his devotion and he blessed the demon with a boon of immortality. This boon made Jalandar to create destruction in the area. The Gods realized the importance to kill the demon to save the mankind. The Gods approached Lord Vishnu to help them in this noble cause. Lord Vishnu understood that the chastity and piousness of Jalandar's wife is acting as a shield to his life. Lord Vishnu adopted the look of Jalandar and started living with his wife. He challenged the chastity of the women and killed Jalandar. When Jalandar's wife Devi Vrinda came to know this, she cursed Lord Vishnu to turn black and ugly. The curse of the lady made him dark and he had to take a holy dip in River Godavari to regain his original form. After regaining his original form, Lord Vishnu was called Sundarnarayan. The temple presents imposing architecture especially the vaulted niche, which strikes a chord with the Mughal sculpture. The east-facing temple has three porches with balcony, lobed arches and globular domes. The main deity Lord Vishnu flanked by Lakshmi and Saraswati is placed in the sanctum. There are small carvings of Hanuman, Narayana and Indira on the walls. The most remarkable thing about this temple is the angle at which it is built. The sun rays of rising sun first fall directly on the idols on March 21st every year. Thousands of devotees visit this temple on this day to witness this holy event. Timings: 6 AM - 12 PM and 5 PM - 9 PM.

Panchavati
Panchavati
Panchavati is a significant religious and pilgrimage site located in Nashik, Maharashtra, India. It holds great importance in Hindu mythology and is believed to be the place where Lord Rama, along with his wife Sita and brother Lakshmana, resided during their exile period. The word "Panchavati" translates to "a garden of five banyan trees" in Sanskrit. It is named after the five ancient banyan trees that are said to have existed in the area. These trees are believed to be symbolic of the five elements of nature. Panchavati is home to several prominent temples and sacred sites. The most renowned among them is the Kalaram Temple, dedicated to Lord Rama. This temple is known for its striking black stone idol of Lord Rama, which is believed to have been self-manifested. Another significant temple in Panchavati is the Sita Gufa (cave), which is believed to be the spot where Sita, the wife of Lord Rama, stayed during their exile. Nearby, there is also the Kapaleshwar Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Panchavati attracts a large number of devotees and tourists throughout the year, especially during the Kumbh Mela, a major Hindu pilgrimage festival that takes place every 12 years in Nashik. The serene and spiritual ambiance of Panchavati, along with its historical and mythological significance, makes it a popular destination for those seeking spiritual solace and cultural exploration.
