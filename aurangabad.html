About Aurangabad

The nearby Shivaji Maharaj Museum, dedicated to the Maratha king Shivaji, displays war weapons and a coin collection. North of the city, the Aurangabad Caves comprise ancient, rock-cut Buddhist shrines. West of the city, battlements surround the medieval Daulatabad Fort. Aurangabad , officially known as Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar, also spelt Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, is a city in the Indian state of Maharashtra. It is the administrative headquarters of Aurangabad district and is the largest city in the Marathwada region. Located on a hilly upland terrain in the Deccan Traps, Aurangabad is the fifth-most populous urban area in Maharashtra after Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur and Nashik with a population of 1,175,116. The city is known as a major production center of cotton textile and artistic silk fabrics. Several prominent educational institutions, including Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, are located in the city. The city is also a popular tourism hub, with tourist destinations like the Ajanta and Ellora caves lying on its outskirts, both of which have been designated as UNESCO World Heritage Sites since 1983. Other tourist attractions include the Aurangabad Caves, Devagiri Fort, Grishneshwar Temple, Jama Mosque, Bibi Ka Maqbara, Himayat Bagh, Panchakki and Salim Ali Lake. Historically, there were 52 Gates in Aurangabad, some of them extant, because of which Aurangabad is nicknamed as the "City of Gates". In 2019, the Aurangabad Industrial City (AURIC) became the first greenfield industrial smart city of India under the country's flagship Smart Cities Mission.

Bibi Ka Maqbara Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar
Bibi Ka Maqbara Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar
Bibi Ka Maqbara, located in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India, is a mausoleum commissioned by the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb in memory of his first wife, Dilras Banu Begum. It bears a striking resemblance to the Taj Mahal and is often referred to as the "Poor Man's Taj" due to its architectural similarities. The mausoleum, built in the late 17th century, showcases Mughal architecture with intricate carvings, delicate lattice work, and a prominent dome.
Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar:
Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar is the historical name for Aurangabad, a city in the state of Maharashtra, India. It was renamed in honor of Chhatrapati Sambhaji, the second ruler of the Maratha Empire and the eldest son of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The city has a rich historical and cultural heritage, with various monuments and landmarks that reflect its significance in Maratha history. Aurangabad is known for its Mughal and Maratha architecture, and it serves as a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors with its historical sites and cultural attractions.
Aurangabad Caves
Aurangabad Caves
The Aurangabad Caves are a group of rock-cut caves situated in Aurangabad, Maharashtra, India. Carved out of soft basalt rock during the 6th and 7th centuries, these caves are primarily Buddhist monastic structures that showcase intricate carvings, sculptures, and artwork. The caves are divided into two main groups ‚Äì the Western Group (Caves 1 to 5) and the Eastern Group (Caves 6 to 10). The Western Group is known for its chaityas (prayer halls) and viharas (monastic cells) and reflects a blend of Buddhist and Hindu architectural styles.
